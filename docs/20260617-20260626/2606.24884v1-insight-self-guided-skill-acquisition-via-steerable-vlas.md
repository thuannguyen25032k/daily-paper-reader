## Abstract
Vision-language-action (VLA) models can learn manipulation skills from demonstrations, but their capabilities are bounded by the skills in the training data. We present InSight, a framework that unlocks autonomous skill acquisition by rendering VLAs steerable at the primitive-action level (e.g., "move gripper to the bowl", "lift upward", "pour the bottle"). InSight consists of two primary stages: (1) an automated segmentation pipeline that partitions demonstrations into labeled primitives via VLM plan decomposition and end-effector poses to enable VLA primitive steerability, and (2) a VLM-guided data flywheel that identifies missing primitives required to accomplish a novel task, autonomously attempts demonstrations of the missing primitives with VLM-proposed low-level control, and automatically labels, stores, and integrates successful demonstrations into the VLA training set. We evaluate InSight across simulation and real-world manipulation tasks, including block flipping, drawer closing, sweeping, twisting, and pouring, without any human demonstrations of these target skills. Once learned, these primitives can be composed to execute novel, long-horizon tasks without additional human demonstrations. Our findings demonstrate that primitive steerability provides a practical foundation for continual skill acquisition in VLA policies. Project website: https://insight-vla.github.io.

- **Motivation:** Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models can learn manipulation skills from human demonstrations, but their capabilities are strictly limited to the skills present in the training data. ### 2. Methodology: Core Idea and Key Technical Details

- **Core Idea:** InSight operates in two stages: (1) automatic primitive segmentation of human demonstrations to train a steerable VLA, and (2) VLM-guided autonomous skill acquisition via a data flywheel that identifies primitive gaps, executes rollouts, verifies success, and retrains the VLA.

- **Primitive Definition:** A primitive is a reusable action segment with a single dominant motion mode (translation or rotation along one axis, or a gripper transition). Each primitive has a natural-language label (e.g., "lift upward", "twist", "pour"). Primitives are segmented from demonstrations, and the VLA is fine-tuned to execute each primitive end-to-end, conditioned on its language label.

- **Stage 1: Automatic Primitive Segmentation**
    - Human demonstrations are collected for known tasks.
    - A VLM generates an ordered sequence of expected primitives (plan).
    - Segment boundaries are identified using gripper open/close transitions and dominant end-effector motion (translation in xy/z, rotation rx/ry/rz).
    - Each primitive segment is conditioned on its label as the language prompt.
    - A learned progress channel (0–1) provides termination signals for each primitive.

- **Stage 2: VLM-Guided Skill Acquisition**
    - **Primitive Gap Identification:** For a novel task, the VLM generates a primitive plan and compares each primitive against the known vocabulary V. Any primitive not in V is flagged as a "primitive gap."
    - **Low-Level Controller:** Each primitive gap is parameterized by a single-axis motion (translational {dx, dy, dz} or rotational {drx, dry, drz}) and a signed magnitude, proposed by a VLM call analyzing the current scene. Chained gaps receive consistent parameters for paired motions (e.g., pour forward then tilt back).
    - **Rollout Execution:** The robot executes the plan—known primitives via the steerable VLA, primitive gaps via the low-level controller.
    - **Success Verification:** A VLM oracle compares before/after images to judge task success. Only successful rollouts are retained.
    - **Retraining:** After n successful rollouts, the VLA is retrained (using LoRA) with the expanded primitive vocabulary, enabling future composition of the new primitives.

### 7. Strengths

- **Novel paradigm:** Shifts VLM role from test-time planner to active agent for persistent skill expansion, enabling continual, lifelong learning without human demonstrations.
- **Compositional structure:** Primitive-level steerability allows new skills to be formed from combinations of known and newly acquired primitives, reducing the need for full-task end-to-end demonstrations.
- **Strong empirical performance:** Consistently outperforms both zero-shot composition baselines (CaP-X) and fine-tuned baselines without new primitives, on both simulation and real-world tasks.
- **Generalizable to OOD states and contact-rich motions:** Demonstrates robustness to distribution shift (drawer closing from open start) and extends beyond prehensile tasks (sweeping).
- **Preserves base skills:** Retraining with new primitives does not degrade performance on original skills (100% retention).

### 8. Limitations

- **Single-axis motion constraint:** The low-level controller for primitive gaps is limited to one axis per gap, restricting the complexity of acquirable primitives. Tasks requiring multi-axis coordinated motions (e.g., complicated assembly) are not supported.
- **No failure analysis:** The system filters only for successful rollouts; there is no mechanism for learning from failures or iterative refinement based on VLM feedback, which could improve sample efficiency.
- **Human environment resets required:** Each rollout requires manual reset of the environment, limiting autonomy and reducing practical scalability.
- **No ablation studies:** The paper does not ablate key design choices (e.g., VLM prompt sensitivity, impact of auto-segmentation quality, effect of progress channel termination). This limits the ability to attribute performance improvements precisely.
- **Limited compute reporting:** Details on GPU hours, training time, and VLM inference cost are absent, making reproducibility and resource estimation difficult.
- **Single-arm manipulation only:** The method is only evaluated on fixed-base manipulators (Franka Panda, UFactory xArm). Extension to mobile manipulators, dual-arm systems, or humanoids is not addressed.
- **Risk of VLM hallucination:** The correctness of primitive gap identification and motion parameter proposal depends on VLM reasoning, which may be unreliable in novel or ambiguous scenarios, potentially causing unsafe or failed executions.
