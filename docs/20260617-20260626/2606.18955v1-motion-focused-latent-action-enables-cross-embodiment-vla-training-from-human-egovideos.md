## Abstract
Training generalist Vision-Language-Action(VLA) models typically requires massive, diverse robotic datasets with high-fidelity action annotations. While egocentric human manipulation videos are abundant and capture significant environmental diversity, the absence of action labels makes them difficult to use in conventional training paradigms. To address this, we propose a latent-action-based framework designed to extract general action priors from unlabeled human videos. The architecture features a Hybrid Disentangled VQ-VAE that decouples motion dynamics from environmental backgrounds through physical masks, enabling the construction of a cross-embodiment action codebook. By pre-training on human videos with the codebook, the VLM backbone learns deep representations of action intent. For adaptation to specific embodiments, we introduce an intent-perception decoupling strategy where the VLM predicts the action intent while a separate frozen visual encoder provides state-specific features to the action expert, thereby reducing action hallucinations. Results in simulation and real-world environments show that our method, pre-trained exclusively on unlabeled human videos, performs competitively with state-of-the-art VLA models trained on massive annotated datasets, requiring only 50 trajectories for downstream adaptation.

## Methodology

This yields a cross-embodiment action codebook that captures embodiment-agnostic motion primitives. The VLM is pre-trained to predict these latent action tokens from visual observations and language instructions. During downstream adaptation, an intent-perception decoupling strategy reduces action hallucinations by separating high-level intent from perceptual feedback.

## Main Conclusions and Findings

- Pre-training exclusively on unlabeled human videos yields performance competitive with SOTA VLA models trained on massive annotated robot datasets (e.g., 91.8% average success rate on LIBERO with our method vs. 90.1% for villa-x).
- Motion-focused latent action significantly improves cross-embodiment alignment (CKA mean 0.9139 vs. 0.8659 for UniVLA), indicating a more consistent action space across different robots and humans.
- Intent-perception decoupling (using frozen DINOv2 instead of VLM embeddings) boosts performance (e.g., from 85.4% to 91.8% on LIBERO average).
- Freezing the VLM during adaptation still yields reasonable performance (52.4% on RoboTwin), suggesting the pre-trained action intentions are highly transferable.
- Downstream adaptation requires only ≈50 trajectories, demonstrating high data efficiency.

## Strengths

- **Novel Use of Unlabeled Human Videos**: The first method to extract and transfer action priors from human egocentric videos without any action annotations, leveraging internet-scale data.
- **Hybrid Disentangled VQ-VAE**: Effective separation of motion from background via physical masks, leading to cleaner latent action representations.
- **Intent-Perception Decoupling**: Reduces action hallucinations by preventing over-reliance on VLM embeddings.
- **Thorough Evaluation**: Covers robot-to-robot, human-to-robot simulation, and real-world dual-arm tasks with multiple baselines and ablations.
- **Representation Analysis**: CKA and latent action visualization provide clear evidence of cross-embodiment consistency.

## Limitations

- **Discrete Codebook Capacity**: The paper acknowledges that the discrete action codebook (size 16, 4 tokens per pair) is insufficient for fine-grained, high-precision manipulation tasks (e.g., tasks requiring millimeter-level accuracy).
- **Mask Dependency**: Relies on segmentation models (SAM2, RoboEngine) for physical masks. Performance may degrade in scenarios with heavy occlusions or segmenting non-human objects.
- **Task Scope**: Evaluated on single-arm and dual-arm manipulation; generalizability to other embodiments (e.g., legged robots, multi-fingered hands) is not demonstrated.
- **Bias Risks**: Pre-training on EgoDex may reflect biases in hand-object interactions (e.g., right-hand dominance, common household objects). Robustness to unseen hand morphologies is uncertain.
- **Resource Reporting**: Lacks details on computational resources and training time, which hinders reproducibility assessment.
