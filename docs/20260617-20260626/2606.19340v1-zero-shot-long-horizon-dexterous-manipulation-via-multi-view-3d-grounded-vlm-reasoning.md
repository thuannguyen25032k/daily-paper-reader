## Abstract
We present a zero-shot framework for long-horizon dexterous manipulation that grounds language instructions into executable 3D task plans from calibrated multi-view RGB images. Rather than training an end-to-end policy, our system uses a vision-language model (VLM) to produce reference-frame task grounding and primitive-level 2D keypoints, then lifts them into 3D via multi-view fusion. This lifting combines triangulation of view-wise VLM groundings with reference-view ray voting, which searches along a semantic camera ray for geometrically consistent candidates across neighboring views. The resulting 3D keypoints support both pick-and-place and tool-use: for tool-use, we retrieve an object-centric atomic action corresponding to the inferred skill category and align its stored 6D tool trajectory to the scene; for dexterous execution, we expand the lifted grasp keypoint into a task-conditioned grasp affordance region and generate feasible grasp-motion pairs with an arm-hand motion generator. Real-world experiments show improved 3D grounding accuracy and execution reliability over single-view RGB-D grounding and fine-tuned VLA baselines. We further demonstrate long-horizon manipulation through closed-loop status verification and replan, enabling zero-shot execution on unseen objects and tool-use tasks in novel scenes.

## 2. Methodology

**Core Idea:**
- Use a VLM to produce **2D keypoints** and task plans from **multi-view RGB images**, then **lift** these into consistent 3D quantities via a fusion of **triangulation** and **reference-view ray voting**.
- For tool-use, retrieve an **object-centric atomic action** from a pre‑built library and align its stored 6D trajectory to the scene.
- For dexterous hand execution, expand the lifted grasp keypoint into a **3D affordance region** and generate feasible grasp-motion pairs using an arm-hand motion generator.

**Key Technical Steps:**

1. **Reference-Frame Grounding**  
   - Given M multi-views and a language instruction, the VLM selects a reference view, infers manipulation mode (pick‑and‑place or tool‑use), and generates a primitive‑level plan with 2D keypoints.

2. **Multi-View 3D Lifting**  
   - For each 2D keypoint, the VLM locates it on all views, then two complementary methods are combined:
     - **Triangulation**: RANSAC‑style pairwise triangulation with reprojection inlier scoring; the candidate with the most views under a threshold ϵtri is accepted if consensus ≥ τtri.
     - **Reference-view ray voting**: Sample Nδ depth candidates along the reference camera ray, project into other views, and let the VLM pick the best index by numbered markers. Aggregate votes across views.
   - The final 3D point is the triangulation result if consensus ≥ τtri, otherwise fall back to the voting result.

3. **Object-Centric Atomic Action Alignment**  
   - For tool‑use tasks, the library contains skill‑categorized 6D trajectories with start/end anchor points.  
   - At test time, the retrieved action is aligned to the scene via a rigid transform Talign computed from the lifted keypoints (apply‑action and terminal points).

4. **Dexterous Affordance-Guided Grasp and Motion Generation**  
   - The lifted grasp keypoint is projected into each view; the VLM predicts a 2D bounding box for the task‑relevant contact region.  
   - A multi‑view inclusion score s(qi) is computed for each mesh vertex of the manipulated object; vertices with score ≥ τ form the 3D affordance region Raff.  
   - Grasp candidates are generated for handle‑like affordances (cylindrical template sampler) or general geometries (optimization‑based, with an affordance objective Laff).  
   - Collision‑aware refinement of placement/terminal points selects the nearest collision‑free position in a local grid.  
   - An off‑the‑shelf motion generator (cuRoboV2) resolves kinematic and collision constraints, yielding a feasible arm‑hand trajectory.

5. **Closed-Loop Verification**  
   - After each execution, the VLM checks progress and decides to continue, retry, or replan within a retry budget (max 4 retries).

## 6. Main Conclusions and Findings

- The proposed multi‑view fusion significantly improves 3D grounding accuracy over single‑view RGB‑D, especially in cluttered scenes (4/5 vs 2/5 on precise P&P).
- Zero‑shot system outperforms fine‑tuned VLA baselines on the tested tasks (0/5 for both VLAs vs. 8–10/10 for Ours).
- Long‑horizon tasks are feasible with closed‑loop retry, though end‑to‑end success rates are moderate (4/6 Organize, 1/3 Cooking).
- Failures stem from arm joint limits, collisions, or unstable grasps, not from 3D grounding errors.

## 7. Strengths

- **Modular and zero‑shot**: no task‑specific training, leverages general VLM knowledge.
- **Robust 3D grounding**: multi‑view fusion with triangulation+ray voting handles occlusion and view ambiguity.
- **Primitive‑level decomposition** enables reuse of atomic actions and easy integration with motion planners.
- **Closed‑loop verification** allows recovery from execution failures in long‑horizon tasks.
- Real‑world validation on diverse tasks with ablation studies.

## 8. Limitations

- **Bounded by VLM reliability**: errors in task decomposition, affordance selection, or 2D grounding propagate to execution failures.
- **Reliance on off‑the‑shelf motion planners**: kinematic singularities, collision‑checker timeouts, and unstable grasps still cause failures.
- **No in‑hand dexterous manipulation**: tasks like rotating an object within the hand, operating scissors, or button pressing are not supported.
- **Atomic action library** is pre‑defined and limited; novel tool‑use patterns not in the library cannot be executed.
- **Evaluation breadth**: VLA baselines tested only on two tasks with small sample sizes; long‑horizon tasks have few trials (3–6). Generalization to more environments or embodiments is not demonstrated.
