## Abstract
Evaluating generalist robot manipulation policies in the real world is expensive, slow, and difficult to scale. Action-conditioned video world models offer a scalable alternative by simulating policy rollouts. Autoregressive rollouts accumulate compounding errors, observations across multiple camera views must remain mutually consistent, and the evaluator must generalize to policies whose behaviors lie outside the training distribution. We address these challenges with SC3-Eval, a self-consistent video generation recipe that adapts a pre-trained video foundation model into an accurate policy evaluator by enforcing three complementary forms of consistency. First, forward-inverse dynamics consistency jointly trains the model to predict frames from actions and to recover actions from frames, anchoring generated rollouts to a physically plausible action manifold and counteracting the drift a forward-only model cannot penalize. Second, cross-view consistency trains the model to inpaint each camera view from the other, keeping the multi-camera observation coherent over long rollouts without any explicit memory mechanism. Third, test-time consistency reuses the inverse dynamics mode at inference as a per-action-chunk uncertainty signal that terminates rollouts whose generated frames drift away from the requested actions. We also demonstrate SC3-Eval rollouts reproduce the failure modes that policies exhibit in real-world rollouts, supporting fine-grained diagnostic comparison rather than aggregate ranking alone. Across seven real-world vision-language-action policies, SC3-Eval attains a closed-loop Pearson correlation of $0.929$ and MMRV of $0.119$, outperforming three strong prior video-model-based baselines, and generalizes to new tasks.

## 2. Methodology: Core Idea and Key Technical Details
- **Core idea:** Enforce self-consistency during training and inference to anchor generated rollouts to physically plausible dynamics and maintain multi-view coherence.
- **Unified dynamics model backbone:** Based on a single transformer (UVA) that shares parameters across forward dynamics (video from actions), inverse dynamics (actions from video), and cross-view inpainting.
- **Three consistency axes:**
  - **Forward-Inverse Dynamics Consistency:** Jointly train forward mode (predict frames from actions) and inverse mode (recover actions from frames). Parameter sharing implicitly regularizes the forward mode to produce frames from which the inverse mode can recover commanded actions, reducing drift.
  - **Cross-View Consistency:** Add a cross-view inpainting mode that randomly selects one camera view and asks the model to inpaint the other views, ensuring long-term multi-view coherence without explicit memory.
  - **Test-Time Consistency (Uncertainty-Driven Early Termination):** At inference, compute per-chunk consistency error \(U_{\text{chunk}}(t) = \frac{1}{l} \sum_{i=t}^{t+l-1} \|a_i - \hat{a}_i\|_2\) where \(\hat{a}_i\) is the inverse mode’s recovered action. Terminate rollout if \(U_{\text{chunk}}(t) > \tau\).
- **Training procedure:** Random mode assignment at each gradient step (80% forward dynamics, 10% cross-view inpainting, 10% inverse dynamics). Loss is a flow-matching objective (rectified flow from Cosmos3 backbone).
- **Inference (Algorithm 1):** Receding-horizon rollout – policy proposes action chunk of length \(l'\), world model generates \(l'\) future frames, only first \(l\) frames are appended; early termination check after each chunk. Horizon decoupling: train at longer horizon \(l' > l\) to preserve pre-training prior and enrich training signal.

## 3. Experiments: Datasets, Scenarios, Benchmarks, Baselines
- **Dataset:** 381-hour real-world table bussing dataset with 12 object categories, three synchronized cameras (two third-person, one wrist), recorded at 20 Hz, subsampled to 10 Hz.
- **Tasks:** In-distribution (table bussing: utensils to bin, trash to can) and out-of-distribution (reverse bussing: swapped destinations, same visuals and motions).
- **Evaluated policies:** Seven π0.5 VLA checkpoints (4 in-distribution, 3 OOD). Each rollout scored on three binary criteria (language following, object lifting, object placing) by blinded human annotators.
- **Metrics:** Pearson correlation \(r\) (linear agreement) and Mean Maximum Rank Violation (MMRV) (rank consistency). Both offline (open-loop, real actions) and online (closed-loop, policy on generated frames).
- **Baselines:** Ctrl-World, IRASim, Cosmos-Predict 2.5 – all re-run under the same protocol.

## 6. Main Conclusions and Findings
- SC3-Eval achieves state-of-the-art evaluation faithfulness: closed-loop Pearson **0.929** and MMRV **0.119**, outperforming all baselines in most settings.
- Generalizes to OOD task (reverse bussing) with only mild degradation.
- Reproduces specific failure modes (language, lifting, placing) with higher reproduction rate than baselines (average 56% vs. 45/36/38% for Ctrl-World/IRASim/Cosmos).
- Each consistency objective contributes: removing inverse dynamics reduces correlation to 0.842, removing cross-view inpainting to 0.802, removing early termination to 0.871, removing horizon decoupling to 0.807.
- Uncertainty-driven early termination is most beneficial on OOD splits; τ=0.02 provides best trade-off.

## 7. Strengths
- **Novel combination of three consistency constraints** that address key failure modes of video world models (drift, multi-view incoherence, distribution shift).
- **Training-free uncertainty signal** from inverse dynamics mode – no extra supervision or ensemble needed for early termination.
- **Prediction-execution horizon decoupling** improves generation quality by leveraging longer pre-training context.
- **Failure mode reproduction** enables diagnostic insights beyond aggregate success rates, not just ranking.
- **Thorough evaluation** across multiple checkpoints, tasks, and ablations; demonstrates generalization to OOD semantics.

## 8. Limitations
- **Inference speed**: 2.3 sec/chunk on a single GB200 GPU – orders of magnitude slower than physics-based simulators, limiting routine use during policy development.
- **Task horizon**: Only validated on short-horizon manipulation (~20 sec episodes). At longer horizons, two failure modes expected: accumulated drift from local corrective signal only, and visual degradation beyond the temporal coherence prior of the pre-trained backbone.
- **Early termination threshold** requires tuning on a held-out subset; performance degrades under aggressive termination on in-distribution splits.
- **Single dataset domain** (table bussing, 12 objects); generalizability to diverse environments not demonstrated.
- **No analysis of calibration** of the uncertainty signal \(U_{\text{chunk}}\) as a calibrated probability; used only as empirical indicator.
