## Abstract
Despite the impressive manipulation capabilities of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models, their operational safety under strict constraints remains largely unverified. To address this, we introduce a parametric safety benchmark to procedurally generate safety-critical scenarios with comprehensive stochasticity. To overcome the scalability bottlenecks of human teleoperation, we develop a novel keypose-driven data generation pipeline. Leveraging this infrastructure, we curate a large-scale dataset of 19,664 strictly collision-free demonstrations with extensive domain randomization. We then conduct a systematic cross-paradigm evaluation of eight VLA and two embodied foundation models. Our analysis reveals a critical generalization-safety tension: although high-diversity training fosters safer trajectories, task success remains fundamentally bottlenecked by sub-optimal trajectory synthesis and semantic misalignment. By providing a scalable pipeline, a robust dataset, and profound failure-mode insights, LIBERO-Safety establishes a crucial foundation for developing safe and reliable VLA models.

# Detailed Summary of LIBERO-Safety: A Comprehensive Benchmark for Physical and Semantic Safety in Vision-Language-Action Models

## 1. Core Problem and Overall Meaning

**Motivation**: Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models are advancing rapidly for general-purpose robotic manipulation, but their operational safety under strict physical and semantic constraints remains largely unverified. Real-world deployment requires reliable collision avoidance, safe human-robot interaction, and the ability to refuse malicious instructions. Existing benchmarks (e.g., LIBERO, VLA-Arena) rely on static environments, lack multi-dimensional safety evaluation, and suffer from scalability bottlenecks due to human teleoperation.

**Objective**: To bridge this gap by introducing **LIBERO-Safety**, a comprehensive benchmark that systematically evaluates both **physical safety** (collision avoidance in cluttered, dynamic environments) and **semantic safety** (reasoning about hazardous instructions) of VLA models. The work also provides a scalable data generation pipeline and large-scale safe demonstration dataset.

## 2. Methodology

**Core Idea**: 
- **Parametric Safety Benchmark**: Uses the **Unified Behavior Domain Definition Language (UBDDL)** to procedurally generate safety-critical scenarios with environmental stochasticity (e.g., camera noise, lighting, object poses, dynamic obstacles). Scenes are parameterized by a tuple ⟨G, Csafety, Sinit, Penv⟩, where Csafety imposes collision-free constraints.
- **Five-Dimensional Safety Taxonomy**: Decouples safety into two tracks:
  - **Physical safety**: Affordance-Aware Grasping (AAG), Human-Robot Interaction (HRI), Tabletop Spatial Avoidance (TSA), Free-Space Hand-Object Avoidance (FSHOA)
  - **Semantic safety**: Semantic Safety Reasoning (SSR) – refusal of harmful instructions.
- Each suite has three difficulty levels (L0–L2), with 5 tasks per level, totaling 75 unique tasks.

**Key Technical Details**:
- **Keypose-driven data generation pipeline**: Human operators define sparse object-centric keyposes (e.g., pre-grasp, grasp) rather than full trajectories. This reduces human effort to 1.8 min/task (vs. 7.4 min for teleoperation). Keyposes are augmented with 5 spatial variants, then the planner CuRobo generates collision-free trajectories under the constraint:
  \[
  A(x(t)) \cap O = \emptyset, \quad \forall t \in [0, T]
  \]
  where \(A\) is the robot volume and \(O\) the obstacle set.
- **Scale**: 19,664 human-screened, collision-free demonstrations across 40 tasks (L0/L1 of four physical suites; L2 and SSR excluded for zero-shot evaluation). Extensive domain randomization (textures, lighting, camera poses, robot initial states) yields wide state-space coverage (Fig. 3).
- **Training dataset**: Excludes all SSR tasks and L2 physical tasks to enable clean zero-shot evaluation of out-of-distribution (OOD) capabilities.

## 3. Experiments

**Environments**: UBDDL-driven simulation with 7,603 unique scenes, 953 objects, 462 hand-object pairs (MANO+GrabNet for hand poses). Dynamic obstacles and human proxies are included.

**Baselines** (10 models across 4 paradigms):
- **Standard VLA**: OpenVLA, OpenVLA-OFT, π0, π0.5
- **WM-based VLA**: UniVLA, VLA-JEPA
- **Dual-System VLA**: GR00T N1.5, GR00T N1.6
- **Embodied Foundation Models** (for SSR only): RoboBrain2.0-7B, RynnBrain-CoP

**Metrics**:
- **Physical track**: Success Rate (SR, requires goal completion without safety violation), Collision Rate (CR), Execution Time, Log-Dimensionless Jerk (LDLJ)
- **Semantic track**: Refusal Rate (RR) and F1 score on balanced sets.

## 4. Compute/Resources

- Training conducted on **8 NVIDIA A800 GPUs** for all models.
- Training steps vary: OpenVLA (200k steps), OpenVLA-OFT (150k), π0/π0.5 (30k), UniVLA (30k), VLA-JEPA (100k), GR00T N1.5 (60k), GR00T N1.6 (20k).
- Specific batch sizes and learning rates are provided in the appendix (Tables A.2–A.9); e.g., π0.5 uses global batch size 256, π0 uses 32.

## 5. Experiment Breadth

- **75 tasks** across both tracks, each evaluated over **10 independent trials** with **3 random seeds** for statistical robustness.
- **Ablation studies**:
  - Data scaling (50 vs. 500 demos per task) using π0.5 and OpenVLA-OFT: more data improves SR and reduces CR.
  - Robustness across 7 perturbation axes (noise, initial state, viewpoint, scene layout, unseen objects, etc.) – reveals sensitivity to spatial reconfigurations.
  - Zero-shot and obstacle-free SFT baselines (Table A.10) to isolate safety-evaluation difficulty.
  - Balanced semantic evaluation (Table A.11) with F1 scores to avoid always-refuse degenerate solutions.
- Coverage is fair: includes diverse models, multi-level difficulty, and systematic perturbations. The benchmark is designed to expose generalization-safety tensions rather than simple performance saturation.

## 6. Main Conclusions and Findings

1. **Standard large-scale pre-training is insufficient** for reactive safety; models fail severely on L2 OOD scenarios.
2. **π0.5 achieves highest overall SR** due to joint training on Internet-scale data and sub-task planning, but still exhibits variance.
3. **High-diversity training mitigates overfitting** and enables emergent spatial reasoning (e.g., direct trajectories in obstacle-free spaces).
4. **Explicit reasoning frameworks (e.g., CoP) are essential** for robust semantic safety alignment; RoboBrain2.0 drops from 80% RR at L0 to 20% at L2.
5. **Scaling demonstration data** yields joint improvements in SR (+2.4%) and CR reduction (12.7% → 10%).
6. **VLA models exhibit resilience to visual perturbations but degrade under global spatial reconfigurations** (layout changes cause CR=6.3% vs. baseline 2.7%).
7. **Failure modes**: Sub-optimal trajectory synthesis (timeouts, kinematic deadlocks) and semantic misalignment (grasping distractors) – task incompletion often decoupled from safety violations.

## 7. Strengths

- **Comprehensive taxonomy**: Covers both physical and semantic safety, with five distinct suites and three difficulty levels, enabling granular analysis.
- **Scalable data generation**: Keypose-driven pipeline reduces human effort 4× and enables 1:M data yield; CuRobo guarantees collision-free trajectories.
- **Cross-paradigm evaluation**: Includes standard, WM-based, dual-system VLAs and embodied foundation models, offering broad architectural insights.
- **Rigorous failure analysis**: Identifies and quantifies the generalization-safety tension, sub-optimal trajectory synthesis, and semantic misalignment as core bottlenecks.
- **Open-source and reproducibility**: All environments, dataset, and evaluation protocols are released; UBDDL enables procedural scene generation.

## 8. Limitations

- **Simulation-only**: The benchmark cannot capture real-world contact dynamics, hardware latency, or unpredictable human behavior. Sim-to-real gap remains unaddressed.
- **Human-in-the-loop keypose collection**: Despite being sparse, keypose design still retains human priors; no automatic end-to-end generation.
- **Training corpus excludes hard-negative unsafe trajectories**: Only safe demonstrations are provided; models are not trained to avoid specific unsafe behaviors (e.g., pushing fragile objects). Future work may need dense unsafe-event annotation.
- **Limited to tabletop manipulation**: The benchmark uses a single robot arm (Franka Panda) in a constrained workspace; does not evaluate mobile manipulation or whole-body safety.
- **Semantic reasoning evaluated zero-shot**: Models are not fine-tuned on the SSR suite; results reflect inherent cognitive alignment but may not show full potential with task-specific training.
- **Static camera intrinsics**: Camera calibration is not randomized; may limit generalization to radical lens distortions.
