## Abstract
Embodied Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models are typically obtained by fine-tuning powerful pretrained VLMs on robotics data, yet it is unclear how much commonsense and factual knowledge they retain after adaptation. Failures on knowledge-sensitive tasks are ambiguous, conflating missing knowledge with poor generalization of low-level control. We introduce Act2Answer, a lightweight protocol that adapts VLM knowledge benchmarks to VLA evaluation by requiring agents to answer through action. Each question becomes a short tabletop episode where the agent performs a single object-placement action to select among candidate answers, yielding an action-grounded success rate with reduced control confounds. We curate a test suite of such environments across diverse commonsense and world-knowledge categories and introduce layerwise intent probing to localize answer-relevant information across the VLM backbone and action head. In a large-scale study of 7 VLA models and 9 VLM baselines, we systematically rank models across categories, finding that VLAs show solid performance on simple concepts while exhibiting larger gaps on richer semantic categories relative to their source VLMs, that VQA co-training is associated with better knowledge retention, and that answer-relevant signals peak in middle VLA layers but attenuate in upper layers. Act2Answer is available at https://tttonyalpha.github.io/act2answer/.

### Methodology
- **Core idea**: Act2Answer converts VLM knowledge questions into short tabletop episodes where the agent selects an answer by performing a minimal object-placement action (placing a cube on the correct image among two candidates). This reduces confounds from long-horizon planning and control difficulty, making the outcome more directly informative about knowledge.
- **Key technical details**:
  - **Binary-choice setup**: Each episode provides two answer images at known positions; the agent must place the cube on the correct one.
  - **Soft Success Rate (SR)**: Success is defined as the cube landing within a tolerance radius ε around the correct image. Formally, SR = 1/N ∑ I(∥p - p+∥ ≤ ε) over N episodes. Three interpretation regimes are defined around a chance baseline of 0.5: instruction/perceptual failure (SR < 0.5 - Δ), no reliable usable knowledge (|SR - 0.5| ≤ Δ), and evidence of usable knowledge (SR > 0.5 + Δ). Δ is computed from binomial sampling fluctuations per category.
  - **Data curation**: 1,720 unique binary questions across 12 knowledge categories (emotion, attribute, state, color, shape, symmetry, counting, time, traffic, public info, celebrity, living world) adapted from VLM benchmarks (MLLM-CompBench, IconQA, MMBench, OK-VQA, VL-Think). Transformations include near-format-preserving, moderate adaptation, and open-ended-to-binary conversion.
  - **Layerwise intent probing**: Linear classifiers are trained on per-layer representations (VLM backbone and Action Expert) to predict the correct answer, quantifying where answer-relevant information is distributed. A Chance-Normalized Retention metric compares the strongest above-chance probing signal in the Action Expert vs. backbone.

2. VLM baselines substantially outperform their VLA counterparts (20–40 point gap) on knowledge-sensitive tasks.
3. VQA co-training (Magma, Xiaomi-Robotics-R0, InternVLA-M1) is associated with better knowledge retention.

### Strengths
- **Controlled protocol**: Act2Answer isolates knowledge from control confounds via minimal embodied action.
- **Systematic benchmark adaptation**: Converts established VLM benchmarks into embodied form, grounding evaluation in validated sources.
- **Comprehensive evaluation**: 7 VLA models, 9 VLM baselines, and extensive ablations (resolution, prompt, rendering, tile size, lighting).

- **Practical insights**: Reveals that continued VQA supervision helps retain knowledge; downstream fine-tuning can be detrimental.

### Limitations
- **Coverage gaps**: Only binary-choice tasks; does not cover complex multi-step or open-ended embodied knowledge use. Limited to tabletop manipulation; navigation or long-horizon tasks not addressed.
- **Bias risks**: Evaluation is entirely in simulation; real-world generalization may differ. The benchmark adapts only a subset of VLM benchmarks; some categories (e.g., temporal, normative) have small numbers of items (e.g., Celebrity only 140 episodes). The adaptation from open-ended VQA (OK-VQA) may change the nature of the task.
- **Application limits**: The protocol is designed for controlled probing, not for assessing full embodied task success in real environments. The assumption that control difficulty is negligible may not hold for all models (e.g., some VLAs struggle with simple placement). The paper does not propose a full mitigation method; preliminary mitigation results are limited.
