## Abstract
Vision-language-action (VLA) policies have shown strong potential for general-purpose manipulation, yet they often fail on novel, out-of-distribution objects whose appearance or geometry deviates from the training distribution. The standard remedy is to collect multi-view teleoperation data for every failure case, but this scales poorly in both cost and time. We introduce Pose6DAug, a failure-driven data augmentation framework that turns a policy's own successful episodes into targeted demonstrations for its failure modes, without any new data collection. Our key insight is that each successful episode already encodes a physically valid action trajectory together with calibrated multi-view observations. By swapping only the manipulated object while preserving this trajectory, we obtain new and physically grounded demonstrations. However, naive 2D video editing breaks multi-view consistency and physical plausibility, particularly under heavy occlusion and egocentric viewpoints. Our method instead operates directly in 3D, anchoring the target object with an explicit mesh driven by a temporally coherent 6D pose trajectory, ensuring geometrically consistent renderings across all camera views. Fine-tuning a VLA on data augmented by our method improves success rates by 16.5% relative to the state-of-the-art baseline on novel objects, while preserving in-distribution performance. These results show that multi-view and physically consistent augmentation is a practical path to scalable VLA generalization.

- **Problem:** Vision-language-action (VLA) policies fail on novel objects due to distribution shifts in appearance and geometry. Standard remedy is collecting multi-view teleoperation data for each failure case, which is expensive and time-consuming.
- **Meaning:** Pose6DAug is a failure-driven data augmentation framework that reuses successful episodes from the base policy to generate targeted demonstrations for its own failure modes without any new data collection. This approach aims to scale VLA generalization cheaply.

## 2. Methodology
- **Core idea:** Operate in 3D space rather than pixels. Given a successful episode with a source object, replace that object with a target object across all camera views while maintaining physical plausibility and multi-view consistency using explicit 3D mesh and temporally coherent 6D pose trajectory.

## 6. Main Conclusions and Findings
- **Primary result:** Fine-tuning VLA on Pose6DAug-augmented data improves success rates on failure episodes by **16.5% relative** over best baseline (VACE), achieving 22.8% average success vs VACE 16.4% and MimicGen 15.8%. Turnover ratio: 24.5% vs 18.2% and 17.2%.
- **Hard object recovery (Table 2):** Pose6DAug recovers 7/8 hardest OOD objects, vs VACE 5/8 and MimicGen 2/8. Success rate on hard examples: 21.2% (Pose6DAug) vs VACE 15.0% and MimicGen 5.7%.
- **In-distribution performance preserved:** On all episodes (Table A1), Pose6DAug achieves highest overall (49.5%), improving OOD from 36.9% to 42.9% while maintaining ID performance (52.7%). MimicGen degrades ID performance (52.2%→52.2% but overall drops due to OOD degradation).
- **Qualitative analysis:** VACE suffers identity drift, multi-view inconsistencies, and incomplete edits. MimicGen produces physically implausible action trajectories for challenging geometries. Pose6DAug maintains consistent identity, geometry, and contact across views.

## 7. Strengths
- **3D-aware, physically grounded:** Explicit mesh and 6D pose ensure geometric consistency across all camera views by construction, avoiding cross-view regularization.
- **Scalable and failure-driven:** Uses any successful episode as source; no simulation rollout failures unlike MimicGen. Works on any source-target pair within same category.
- **Preserves action trajectory integrity:** By leveraging original successful trajectory, augmented videos contain physically valid motions and grasps up to rescaling.
- **Improves generalization on novel objects:** Substantially outperforms 2D generation and simulation-based baselines without sacrificing in-distribution performance.
- **Simple augmentation strategies:** Rotation, translation, scaling effectively increase diversity while remaining physically plausible.

## 8. Limitations
- **Dependence on external perception models:** Relies on SAM3D for mesh reconstruction and VOID for background inpainting; inherits their failure modes (incomplete meshes, inpainting artifacts).
- **Grasp compatibility constraints:** Replacing objects of drastically different scale/geometry can lead to implausible grasps; no automated affordance matching.
- **Lighting interaction not modeled:** Direct rendering onto inpainted backgrounds may not capture dynamic shadows, reflections, or complex lighting; no neural relighting.
- **Simulation-only evaluation:** Experiments conducted solely on RoboCasa365; real-world domain gaps (e.g., camera calibration noise, pose estimation errors) not addressed.
- **Requires calibrated cameras and known extrinsics:** Assumes calibrated multi-view setup; may not directly transfer to uncalibrated or changing viewpoints.
- **Cost of initial successful episodes:** Still requires at least one successful demonstration per source object; for entirely novel categories, no source exists.
- **Limited scope of tested perturbations:** Only rotation, translation, scaling along approach axis; other physically plausible modifications (e.g., shape deformation, partial occlusion) not explored.
